--- Title

QGIS: introduction to mapping

--- Summary

Create a custom map with a professional open source program

--- Details (HTML)

<p>
	QGIS is an open source Geographic Information System (GIS) that can be used to manipulate, visualise, analyse and publish spatial data.
</p>
<p>
	The library's Digital Scholars Hub (DSH) offers a short introduction directed at beginners.
</p>
<p>
	In this hands-on session, you will learn to:
</p>
<ul>
	<li>use vector and raster layers</li>
	<li>access a few sources of spatial data</li>
	<li>amend the data and add to it</li>
	<li>personalise the look of the map</li>
	<li>compose a print-out of your map</li>
</ul>
<p>
	The program is available on the Library's training computers, but you are welcome to bring you own laptop. If you do so, 
	<em>please install the software beforehand</em>: it is available on the <a href="https://qgis.org/en/site/forusers/download.html">QGIS website</a> (version 3.4 or above).
</p>
