--- Title

OpenStreetMap: contribute and use the data

--- Summary

Learn how to contribute geographical data to OpenStreetMap, and use the database in QGIS.

--- Details (HTML)

<p>
	OpenStreetMap (OSM) is an open-licence spatial database.
</p>
<p>
	In this hands-on session, you will learn how to:
</p>
<ul>
	<li>Digitise geographical data into the OSM database</li>
	<li>Use an editor directly from the browser</li>
	<li>Contribute local data as well as data useful to humanitarian action across the world</li>
	<li>Make use of OSM data in QGIS to create a custom map</li>
</ul>
<p>
	You do not need any prior knowledge to attend this session.
</p>
<p>
	<em>Before the session</em>, please have a look at the <a href="https://www.openstreetmap.org/" target="_blank">OpenStreetMap website</a> and make note of something that is missing near your home!
</p>
<p>
	The software is available on the Library's training computers, but if you want to work on your own laptop, please <a href="https://qgis.org/en/site/forusers/download.html">install QGIS</a> before coming to the session (version 3.4 or above).
</p>
