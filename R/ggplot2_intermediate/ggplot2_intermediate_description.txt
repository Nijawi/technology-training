--- Title of session

R data visualisation with RStudio and ggplot2: intermediate

--- Summary

Further customise your R data visualisations with RStudio and ggplot2

--- Details (HTML)

<p>
	The Library is offering a free R intermediate session on graphics using the 
	<strong>ggplot2</strong> package.
</p>
<p>
	During this hands-on session, you will:
</p>
<ul>
	<li>install extra tools for picking colours</li>
	<li>create a custom colour palette</li>
	<li>customise scales and ranges</li>
	<li>divide a visualisation into facets</li>
	<li>explore new geometries</li>
	<li>adjust a geometry's position</li>
	<li>further modify themes</li>
</ul>
<p>
	The software is available on the training room's computers. If you prefer to use your own laptop, you are also welcome to bring it in (but please install the software beforehand, or come 15 minutes earlier if you haven't).
</p>
<p>
	To install R and RStudio on your own laptop, refer to 
	<a href="https://gitlab.com/stragu/DSH/blob/master/R/Installation.md">our instructions</a>.
</p>
<p>
	You should be familiar with R and ggplot2 to attend this session. It is recommended (but not essential) to have followed the "R data visualisation with RStudio and ggplot2" introductory session before attending this one (the 
	<a href="https://gitlab.com/stragu/DSH/blob/master/R/ggplot2_intro/ggplot2_intro.md">written material</a> and a <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LoeBgXSJWTw">screencast</a> are 
	available online).
</p>
<p>
	If you are interested in other R sessions, please search for "RStudio" on StudentHub or see the list of events on the Library's 
	<a href="https://web.library.uq.edu.au/library-services/training">training page</a>.
</p>
